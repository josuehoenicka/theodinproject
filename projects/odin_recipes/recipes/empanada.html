Empanada (from Venezuela)

Venezuelan empanadas are a popular type of pastry that is stuffed with a variety of fillings and then deep-fried until crispy. The dough is made with flour, water, and salt, and the filling can be made with meat, cheese, beans, vegetables, or a combination of these. Empanadas are often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer and are a staple in Venezuelan cuisine.

  • All-purpose flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil for frying
  • Any desired filling (e.g. meat, cheese, beans, vegetables, etc.)
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Add warm water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together and is smooth.
  3. Let the dough rest for 30 minutes to 1 hour to allow it to relax and become easier to work with.
  4. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each out into a circle, about 6-8 inches in diameter.
  5. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle, leaving enough space around the edges to seal the empanada.
  6. Fold the dough over to enclose the filling and crimp the edges with a fork or your fingers to seal.
  7. Heat oil in a large pot or deep-fryer to 350-375Â°F.
  8. Carefully add the empanadas to the hot oil and fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  9. Remove the empanadas with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Venezuelan empanadas!
