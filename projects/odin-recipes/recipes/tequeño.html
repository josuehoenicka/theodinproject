Tequeño (from Venezuela)

Venezuelan Tequeños are a popular finger food that is made by wrapping a stick of cheese in dough and then frying until crispy. The dough is made with flour, water, salt, and sometimes a little sugar, and the cheese filling can be made with a variety of cheeses such as queso blanco or queso de mano. Tequeños are often served as an appetizer or snack and are a beloved part of Venezuelan cuisine.

  • All-purpose flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Sugar (optional)
  • Oil for frying
  • Any desired cheese filling (e.g. cheese, custom cheese, etc.)
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar (if using).
  2. Add warm water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together and is smooth.
  3. Let the dough rest for 10-15 minutes to allow it to relax and become easier to work with.
  4. Divide the dough into small pieces, about the size of a golf ball.
  5. Roll each piece of dough into a long, thin strip, about 1/4 inch in diameter.
  6. Wrap the dough around a stick of cheese, leaving a little bit of space between each layer of dough.
  7. Press the ends of the dough together to seal and trim any excess dough.
  8. Heat oil in a large pot or deep-fryer to 350-375°F.
  9. Carefully add the tequeños to the hot oil and fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  10. Remove the tequeños with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  11. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Venezuelan tequeños!
