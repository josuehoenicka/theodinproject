Arepa (from Venezuela)

Venezuelan arepas are a popular food in Venezuela made from corn flour (Harina P.A.N), water, and salt. The dough is formed into patties, cooked on a griddle or skillet with oil or butter, and then split open to be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as cheese, meat, beans, avocado, and more. Arepas can be eaten any time of day and are often enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. They are a staple in Venezuelan cuisine and are enjoyed all over the country, as well as in other Latin American countries and around the world.

  • Corn flour (Harina P.A.N)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil or butter for cooking
  • Any desired filling (e.g. cheese, meat, beans, avocado, etc.)
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the corn flour and salt.
  2. Add warm water and mix until the dough comes together and is smooth.
  3. Let the dough rest for a few minutes to allow the flour to absorb the water.
  4. Divide the dough into equal portions and form each into a ball.
  5. Flatten each ball into a patty, about 1/2 inch thick.
  6. Heat a griddle or skillet over medium-high heat and brush with oil or butter.
  7. Cook the arepas on each side until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes per side.
  8. Remove from the heat and let cool for a few minutes.
  9. Using a knife, carefully split each arepa in half to create a pocket for the filling.
  10. Fill the arepas with your desired ingredients, such as cheese, meat, beans, avocado, and more.
  11. Enjoy your delicious Venezuelan arepas!
